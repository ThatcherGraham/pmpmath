CONCLUSION:

The major difference here is that the real AV is about half of the figure produced by the PM formula, it is a number very similar to the mean. There is a reason for that difference. Francis Galton invented the concepts of variance and standard deviation in the 1860s to best measure typical variance. These were sets of random data such as guesses. This is particulary applicable to our use. Galton found that the mean, mode and median all had relationships to the correct answer. The popular PM method is of uncertain origin. You can't add sum or average standard deviations like other numbers, and it covers that basic requirement.
